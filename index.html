The Legend

Born in the Year of the Fire Horse

Three days. Three paths. One fire to follow.

In Chinese astrology, the Fire Horse arrives only once every sixty years — passionate, untameable, luminous. Those born under its sign carry a rare fire: bold, magnetic, and wholly alive.

In 1966, a remarkable group of Fire Horses entered the world. In 2026, that same fire turns sixty — and we are gathering in the mountains of Winthrop, Washington to celebrate exactly as Fire Horses should: with music, dancing, horses, good food, and people we love.

Three days. Three unforgettable experiences. One trifecta weekend for the ages.

Getting There

Winthrop, Washington

🗺️

Getting to Winthrop

North-central Washington in the Methow Valley. From Seattle: ~3.5 hours over Highway 20 — check pass conditions in early May. From Spokane: ~3 hours heading west.

🏨

Where to Stay

Winthrop Inn, Sun Mountain Lodge (a splurge worth making), and numerous vacation rentals on VRBO and Airbnb. Book early — May in Winthrop fills up fast.

🌡️

Weather in Early May

Expect variability. Pack layers regardless of forecast. The Sunday hike will be cooler at elevation — bring a jacket for the summit.

🍺

Three Finger Jack's

Our Derby Day home — a classic Winthrop pub in the heart of the Old West downtown. Food and drinks fully hosted on Saturday.

🛼

Twisp Community Center

Located in Twisp, just 8 miles south of Winthrop. Easy drive or carpool for Friday Disco Night.

🏔️

Patterson Lake Trailhead

Meet at noon Sunday at the Patterson Lake parking lot. Moderate hike with stunning Methow Valley summit views.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Is there a cost to attend?

No — all three events are hosted. Food and drinks provided at Derby Day. Snacks at Disco Night (BYOB for drinks). Bring yourself, your outfit, and something to share at the Sunday picnic.

Do I have to come to all three days?

Not at all — come to whatever works. Derby Day on Saturday is the main event if you can only make one. The full trifecta is the intended experience though.

Is the hat on Derby Day really required?

Required is a strong word. Strongly encouraged is an understatement. There is a Best Hat Contest with prizes. This is Kentucky Derby — the hat is the thing. Please bring a hat.

What time does the actual Kentucky Derby run?

Post time is 3:57 PM Pacific Time on Saturday May 2nd. We'll be watching it live at Three Finger Jack's. Be there well before 3:57 — the post parade and My Old Kentucky Home are part of the experience.

What should I bring to the Sunday hike?

Comfortable hiking shoes, layers for the summit, sunscreen, water, and something to share for the picnic. Moderate difficulty — take your time and enjoy the views.

I have dietary restrictions — will there be options?

The Derby Day feast is a full Southern spread with plenty of variety. Mention restrictions in your RSVP and we'll take care of you.

Who are the hosts?

This celebration is hosted by Abbey, Donna Baille, and Eileen Mackusick. Reach Abbey at 206-713-1720 or abbeylinh@gmail.com.
