Game One

The Stick Pony Calcutta

Bid on a pony. Cheer like you own it. Because you do.

Before the heats run, all 9 stick ponies are auctioned to the highest bidder in the room. Every bid goes into one pot. After the race, the pot pays the people who bid on the win, place, and show ponies — and the pony teams themselves get a cut.

How It Works

01

Get Your Paddle

Every guest receives an auction paddle at sign-in. Yours to bid with.

02

The Auction

Dave Johnston auctions all 9 ponies one by one. Bid solo or pool with friends.

03

The Race

Three heats, then a final. Win, Place, Show — the same as Churchill Downs.

04

Cash Out

Cash payouts immediately after the race finishes.

Game Two

The Kentucky Derby Pool

Buy a ticket. Draw a horse. Pray to the Run for the Roses.

A weighted fishbowl draw on the actual Kentucky Derby. $10 buys a ticket, you draw a horse, and if your horse finishes Win / Place / Show — you get paid. Mike has weighted the horses by morning-line odds, so favorites get more tickets and longshots get fewer. Buy as many as you want.

How It Works

01

Buy at the Door

$10 per ticket. Buy one or buy ten — your odds, your call.

02

Draw a Horse

Reach into the fishbowl, pull a slip. That horse is yours for the race.

03

Watch the Derby

3:57 PM, NBC. The 152nd Run for the Roses live at Churchill Downs.

04

Collect the Cash

Win, Place, or Show finishers split the pot 60 / 25 / 15.

Game Three

The Exacta Squares

Pick a square. Wait for fate. Take the whole damn pot.

If you've ever played Super Bowl squares, you already know how this works. $0.50 per square. You claim squares first — then horses are randomly assigned to the rows and columns. The square where the actual 1st-place horse and 2nd-place horse intersect wins the entire pot.

How It Works

01

Claim Squares

Find Mike's table. $0.50 per square. Take as many as you want.

02

Random Draw

Once the grid fills, horses are drawn randomly and assigned across the top (1st place) and down the side (2nd place).

03

The Derby Runs

Only the actual 1st-place and 2nd-place finishers matter.

04

Winner Takes All

The square where 1st place column meets 2nd place row wins the full pot.

Rules of the Squares

  • $0.50 per square. Claim as many squares as you want.
  • Horses are assigned randomly after all squares are claimed — you can't pick your horses.
  • Winner takes the entire pot — no Win / Place / Show split.
  • If no one's square exactly matches the actual 1st-and-2nd finish, the closest match wins.
  • Cash only. Mike runs the entire square pool.
