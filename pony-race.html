What You're Signing Up For

The centerpiece silly moment of Derby Day. Two-person teams race a stick pony around a 390-foot loop on Riverside Ave — one rider galloping the pony in front, one coconut clapper following close behind providing authentic Monty Python hoofbeat sounds. Costumes encouraged. Dignity optional.

The Course

Start at Three Finger Jack's, run up Riverside Ave, cross the street, and gallop back to Jack's. Spectators watch from the pub patio and along the sidewalk — front row seats to both the start and the finish.

390-foot loop · start and finish at Three Finger Jack's

The Rules

Team Formation

  • Rider in front, clapper behind — every team, every heat.
  • Stay within 3 feet of each other for the entire race. The clapper must keep within 3 feet of the rider — fall behind and your team earns demerits in the scoring.

Race Conduct

  • No regular running — this is a stick pony gallop, not a sprint. Move like you mean it, but stay in character.
  • No blocking or checking other teams.
  • No cutting corners — stay on the marked course.
  • Spotters are watching for the 3-foot rule, lane breaks, false starts, and corner cutting. Each heat has its own spotter.

Quick Questions

Do I need my own stick pony?

No — we supply the ponies, jockey hats, bibs, and coconuts. You supply the team and the enthusiasm.

What if I can't find a coconut clapper?

Sign up anyway and note their name as TBD. There are always extras at parties like this who love a good silly moment — we'll help pair you up.

What about prizes?

Yes — there's prize money for the top three teams. Details will be shared with registered teams in the days leading up to the event.

Costume requirements?

None. But — this is a Kentucky Derby / Year of the Fire Horse 60th birthday party at a pub in Winthrop. Read the room.
