What You're Signing Up For

The centerpiece silly moment of Derby Day. Two-person teams race a decorated stick pony around a 390-foot loop on Riverside Ave — one rider galloping the pony, one coconut clapper providing authentic Monty Python hoofbeat sounds. Costumes encouraged. Dignity optional.

The Course

Start at Three Finger Jack's, run up Riverside Ave, cross the street, and gallop back to Jack's. Spectators watch from the pub patio and along the sidewalk — front row seats to both the start and the finish.

Quick Questions

Do I need my own stick pony?

No — we supply the ponies. You supply the team and the enthusiasm. Bring decorations if you want to go all-out.

What if I can't find a coconut clapper?

Sign up anyway and note their name as TBD. There are always extras at parties like this who love a good silly moment — we'll help pair you up.

Are kids allowed to race?

Bring 'em on. Just confirm with Abbey if you want to sign up a youth team.

What about prizes?

Yes — there's prize money for the top three teams. Details will be shared with registered teams in the days leading up to the event.

Costume requirements?

None. But — this is a Kentucky Derby / Year of the Fire Horse 60th birthday party at a pub in Winthrop. Read the room.

Betting details (Calcutta auction + Derby raffle) coming soon — registered pony teams will be the first to know.
